— The featured moment —

Pitch your friends.

If you're showing up with a single friend, here's the deal: at 6:30 we hand the mic around. Couples and friends get two minutes to hype up the single they brought. Their best qualities, their dealbreakers, the one thing the room should know.

It's the warmest part of the night. Singles get an actual intro instead of a swipe. Couples get to play matchmaker out loud. The room finds out who's worth talking to before anyone has to do the awkward approach themselves.

07
— Questions —

You probably want to ask.

Is this speed dating?

No. There's no rotating chairs, no buzzer, no timer on your conversation. It's a beer hall full of people who are open to meeting people, with name tags and color stickers so you don't have to ask awkward questions. You can mingle, sit with your friends, ignore everyone — it's a Friday night.

I'm in a couple. Can I come?

Yes — and you should. The whole concept is "friends of friends." Couples and partners are the best matchmakers. Bring the single friend you've been meaning to set up for the last three years. Wear a red sticker on your name tag so single people know.

Is this LGBTQ+ inclusive?

Fully. Every gender and orientation is welcome — the sticker legend is intentionally orientation-neutral so nobody has to label anything they don't want to label. The Ally sticker exists for anyone here in support, including LGBTQ+ allies who want to be part of the night without being part of the dating market.

Why "Dating in person"?

Because the best couples we know all started with a friend introducing them at a Friday-night thing. This is a bigger version of that — your group chat scaled up to a beer hall.

What's the catch?

None. It's free, it's 21+, no cover. We cap RSVPs at 100 because the curation is the whole point — we want a room full of friends-of-friends, not a public bar event.

Do I have to RSVP?

Yes — please. We're capped at 100, and it helps Schoolyard plan staffing and Fresh plan their chair-side work. Walk-ins are welcome only if we haven't hit cap.

Is it 21+?

Yes. Schoolyard is a beer hall. Bring ID.

What should I wear?

Whatever you'd wear to a beer hall on a Friday night. We're not running a gala. Jeans are a yes. Cute top is a yes. The shirt you stole from your roommate is a yes. If you can't be trusted with your hair, Fresh has a chair on-site for touch-ups.

What if I don't know anyone?

Then this is the event for you. Friends of friends means every group started with someone who showed up alone. Grab a name tag, grab a drink, you'll be talking to someone in five minutes.

Where exactly is Schoolyard?

Schoolyard Beer Hall, Denver. Full address and a Google Maps pin go out in the RSVP confirmation email. If you've ever ended up there on a Friday, you already know the room.

How do I get there?

Lyft from most central Denver neighborhoods runs about ten to fifteen minutes — usually under twenty bucks if you split it. Street parking around the venue is free but fills up fast on Fridays; plan for a five-minute walk if you're driving. We'll send turn-by-turn details in the confirmation email.
